About This Project

Vegetative Area Analysis using Satellite Imagery

Explore vegetation health, monitor changes across seasons, and visualize ecological insights using satellite data. This page explains our mission, the science behind the metrics, and the workflow that turns pixels into decisions.

Mission We make vegetation monitoring accessible, accurate, and visually clear for land managers, researchers, and communities.
Approach We combine open satellite data with reproducible processing and intuitive design to deliver actionable maps.
Focus Areas Agriculture, forestry, wetlands, restoration projects, urban greenery, and disaster impact assessment.

Why Vegetation Analysis?

Five practical outcomes that stakeholders care about.

Crop Health & Yield
Detect stress early, optimize irrigation and fertilization, and forecast yield.
Forest Monitoring
Track canopy density, fragmentation, and post-disturbance recovery.
Change Detection
Quantify seasonal cycles and long‑term trends in vegetation health.
Disaster Assessment
Estimate burn severity, drought impact, and flood recovery at scale.
Urban Green Spaces
Measure canopy cover and support equitable, climate‑resilient planning.

Key Vegetation Metrics

We use well‑known spectral indices and biophysical proxies.

NDVI — Normalized Difference Vegetation Index
Greenness proxy from red & NIR bands.
(NIR - Red) / (NIR + Red)
EVI — Enhanced Vegetation Index
Improves sensitivity in high biomass areas.
G * (NIR - Red) / (NIR + C1*Red - C2*Blue + L)
NDWI — Normalized Difference Water Index
Moisture indicator using NIR & SWIR.
(NIR - SWIR) / (NIR + SWIR)
LAI — Leaf Area Index (modelled)
Canopy structure proxy derived from reflectance/indices.
Burn Severity (dNBR)
Pre‑/post‑fire NBR difference.
NBRpre − NBRpost

From Pixels to Insights: Workflow

1) Data Sourcing
Sentinel‑2, Landsat, and commercial imagery; cloud & QA masks included.
2) Pre‑processing
Atmospheric correction, resampling, compositing, and cloud/shadow removal.
3) Index Computation
NDVI/EVI/NDWI and other band math computed per scene or composite.
4) Change Analysis
Time‑series smoothing, anomalies vs. baseline, and thresholds.
5) Visualization & Reporting
Interactive maps, charts, and exportable summaries for stakeholders.

FAQ

Which satellites are supported?
Commonly Sentinel‑2 (10m), Landsat 8/9 (30m), and others where licensing permits.
How often can I get updates?
Every 5–10 days for Sentinel‑2 in many regions; daily if fusing multiple sources.
Do clouds ruin the analysis?
We apply cloud/shadow masks and temporal compositing to reduce noise.
Can I export the results?
Yes—maps, charts, and tabular summaries (CSV/GeoJSON/PNG) can be exported.
